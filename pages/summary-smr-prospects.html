Summary of "Exploring the potential of micro nuclear reactors for electrifying developing regions" [sic]

Opening Notes: This is a summary of an article regarding The use of nuclear microreactors to serve developing regions. This summary is quite a bit shorter than my previous work because the work I summarized is only about a third as long. Citations are at the bottom of the page.

Nuclear microreactors (nuclear reactors that could fit into, for example, a truck) could be a useful method of getting electricity to areas without it, but pose risks in terms of the safety in conflict-ridden areas or areas without trained personnel to manage them. In order to quantify this, a recent report attempted to determine what percent of these areas could be served by microreactors. The report found the following:

  1. 80% of the land theoretically could be served by an microreactor.
  2. 87% of the population would be better served by renewable electricity.
  3. · Government issues would make construction of a nuclear reactor unwise for 80% of the remaining population.

Overall, nuclear power isn’t the right choice for around 98% of these areas for now.
