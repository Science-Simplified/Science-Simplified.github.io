Summary of "Technology Development of a High-Capacity High-Assay Low Enriched Uranium Transportation Concept"

Opening Notes: This is a summary of a recent paper on a new method to transport reactor-grade uranium. It goes by each part of the paper excluding the abstract and replacing the final sections with an "Other Information" section. Citations are at the botton of the page.

Introduction:
  • High Assay Low Enrichment Uranium (HALEU) is uranium with an increased percentage of uranium-235, specifically made of 5% to 20% Uranium-235.
  • The USA needs to transport HALEU fuel cheaply and efficiently.
  • This concept was created during a workshop run by the Idaho National Laboratory and other nuclear energy research organizations to advice the U.S. Department of Energy on various nuclear energy concepts including the transportation of HALEU.
Background:
  • U.S. nuclear reactor concepts use HALEU fuel in the form of Uranium Dioxide, but there are very few places in the country to get HALEU.
  • Transporting HALEU is difficult because of regulations which ensure that large amounts of radioactive material is stored and transported safely.
A High Capacity HALEU Transportation Concept:
  • This concept modifies an existing high capacity transportation package, namely the Nuclear Assurance Company Optimal Modular Universal Shipping system for Low-Activity Contents (OPTIMUS-L).
  • This modified version contains two sections: the basket, which contains the HALEU, and the flux trap, which absorbs neutrons in order to help prevent a nuclear chain reaction.
  • One of these packages could transport up to 376 kg (829 lbs) of HALEU
  • Five of these can be placed on one truck, for a total weight of 1,881 kg (4149 lbs) of HALEU per truck.
Safety Evaluations and Results:
  • This concept was evaluated and found to pass safety standards in terms of criticality safety (likelihood of a nuclear chain reaction), radiological safety (safety of radioactive emission from the package), heat tolerance, and packaging operation feasibility (how reasonable it is to actually use this packaging system).
  • The concept had some issues with the structural integrity in simulated fall conditions, but the authors feel that a few optimizations could fix this.
Other Information:
  • This technology is now patented and will be exclusively licensed to an unnamed-by-the-authors “interested party”
  • Follow-up research may focus on other uranium based compounds, like uranium tetrafluoride.

Citation:
Eidelpes, Elmar Ferdinand, Jarrell, Josh J, Bertsch, Denise Nicole, Hall, Robert, Marshall, William, Hom, Brian, & Adkins, Harold. Technology Development of a High-Capacity High-Assay Low Enriched Uranium Transportation Concept. United States.
